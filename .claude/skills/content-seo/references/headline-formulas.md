# Headline Formulas — Contractor Websites

## How to Use This Reference

These formulas are battle-tested patterns for contractor H1s, H2s, section headlines, and CTAs. For each formula: apply the pattern, fill in the contractor's specifics, then verify it passes the three-part test:

1. **Specific outcome?** (What will the visitor get or have fixed?)
2. **Speed or convenience signal?** (When? How easy?)
3. **Trust qualifier?** (Why this contractor?)

If a headline fails two or more parts of this test, rewrite it.

---

## Formula 1 — Outcome + Location + Trust

**Pattern:** `[Service result] in [City] — [Trust signal]`

**Examples:**
- "Water Heater Installed in Sacramento — Same-Day, Licensed & Insured"
- "Roof Replaced in San Jose — Manufacturer-Certified, 50-Year Warranty"
- "AC Repaired in Fresno — NATE-Certified, 24/7 Emergency Service"
- "Electrical Panel Upgraded in Oakland — Permitted Work, NEC Compliant"

**Best for:** H1 headlines on service pages and service area pages

**Avoid:** Don't stack more than two trust signals in one headline — it reads like a list, not a promise.

---

## Formula 2 — Problem → Fix → Timeline

**Pattern:** `[Problem] Fixed [timeframe] by [Trust signal]`

**Examples:**
- "Burst Pipe? Fixed Today by Sacramento's Licensed Plumbers"
- "No Heat in Winter? Same-Day Furnace Repair by Certified HVAC Techs"
- "Roof Leaking After the Storm? Emergency Repairs, 24-Hour Response"
- "Lights Flickering? Diagnosed and Fixed Same Day — Licensed Electrician"

**Best for:** Emergency service pages, homepage hero for 24/7 contractors, service area page heroes

**Avoid:** Don't use "?" followed by a weak answer. The fix statement must be as strong as the problem is vivid.

---

## Formula 3 — Number + Social Proof

**Pattern:** `[Number] [City] Homeowners Trust [Company] for [Service]`
OR: `[Number]-Star [Service] in [City]`

**Examples:**
- "Serving Sacramento Since 2006 — Over 2,000 Plumbing Jobs Completed"
- "4.9-Star Roofing in San Diego — 500+ Verified Reviews"
- "Sacramento's Highest-Rated HVAC Company — 847 Five-Star Reviews"
- "18 Years. Thousands of Jobs. One Sacramento Plumber You Can Count On."

**Best for:** Homepage hero subheadline, testimonials section headline, trust bar section

**Avoid:** Don't inflate numbers. If the review count is 23, don't use this formula for the review count — use years in business or jobs completed instead.

---

## Formula 4 — Direct Address + Urgency

**Pattern:** `[Situation]? We'll [specific action] [timeframe].`

**Examples:**
- "No Hot Water? We'll Have It Running Again Today."
- "AC Down? We'll Have a Tech at Your Door Within 4 Hours."
- "Roof Leaking? We'll Be On-Site Within 2 Hours — Guaranteed."
- "Electrical Emergency? We'll Respond Tonight."

**Best for:** Emergency service H2s, sticky banner CTAs, above-the-fold section on 24/7 pages

**Avoid:** Only use this formula when the response time promise is real and backed by `siteConfig.contact.responseTime`. Never publish a promise the contractor can't keep.

---

## Formula 5 — Comparison / Differentiation

**Pattern:** `[What others do] vs. [What we do] — The Difference Is [Proof]`
OR simplified: `Not [vague competitor behavior]. [Specific better behavior].`

**Examples:**
- "No Guessing. No Upselling. Just an Honest Diagnosis and a Fair Price."
- "We Quote Before We Wrench — No Surprise Bills."
- "Other Roofers Disappear After Installation. We Warranty Our Work for 10 Years."
- "We Won't Send an Apprentice. Every Job Gets a Licensed Electrician."

**Best for:** Homepage "Why Choose Us" section, About page intro, service page differentiator callout

**Avoid:** Never name competitors explicitly. This formula works on the category level, not the competitor level.

---

## Formula 6 — Question Headline (AEO-Optimized)

**Pattern:** `[Question a homeowner would ask] — [Direct answer in headline]`

**Examples:**
- "How Much Does a New Water Heater Cost in Sacramento? [$900–$1,800 Installed]"
- "How Long Does a Roof Replacement Take? [1–2 Days for Most Homes]"
- "Is It Worth Repairing an Old AC Unit? [Here's How to Decide]"
- "Do I Need a Permit to Replace My Electrical Panel? [Yes — We Handle It]"

**Best for:** FAQ section H3s, blog post titles, service page H2s that answer common questions

**Avoid:** Don't answer a question vaguely — if the answer to "how much" is "it depends," write a paragraph about what it depends on, not just "it depends."

---

## Formula 7 — Process / Steps Headline

**Pattern:** `Our [Service] Process — [Number] Steps to [Outcome]`
OR: `How We [Service]: [Outcome] Every Time`

**Examples:**
- "Our Plumbing Repair Process — 4 Steps from Call to Fix"
- "How We Replace a Roof: Clean Site, Tight Installation, Final Inspection — Every Time"
- "The [Business Name] HVAC Installation Process: Assessment, Install, Test, Done"
- "3 Steps to a Clean Home: Book, We Clean, You Relax"

**Best for:** Service page "process" section headline, About page methodology section

**Avoid:** Don't claim a "process" that's just a bulleted list of obvious steps. The process section only earns its keep if the steps differentiate the contractor (e.g., "we photograph the before and after" or "you get a written scope before we start").

---

## Formula 8 — Guarantee Headline

**Pattern:** `[Specific guarantee] — Or [Consequence we bear]`

**Examples:**
- "If It's Not Fixed Right, We Come Back Free — Every Time, No Exceptions"
- "On Time or the Service Call is Free"
- "We Guarantee Your Roof for 10 Years — Leaks, Materials, Labor"
- "100% Satisfaction Guarantee: Love the Clean or We Come Back"

**Best for:** Trust section headline, homepage CTA section, About page credibility section

**Avoid:** Vague guarantees ("100% satisfaction guaranteed") with no consequence. The formula only works if the "or what" is specific.

---

## Formula 9 — Seasonal / Situational Urgency

**Pattern:** `[Season/situation] in [City]? Don't Wait — [Time-sensitive consequence]`

**Examples:**
- "Sacramento Summers Hit 110°F — Get Your AC Ready Before It Does"
- "Before the Rainy Season: Is Your Roof Ready for California's Winter Storms?"
- "Selling Your Home? A Pre-Listing Inspection Catches Problems Before Buyers Do"
- "Frozen Pipes Don't Wait for Monday — 24/7 Emergency Response"

**Best for:** Seasonal blog posts, homepage banner for seasonal promotion, service pages for seasonal services

**Avoid:** Don't use seasonal urgency year-round — it loses credibility. Time-box seasonal copy or use conditional rendering.

---

## Formula 10 — Local Authority

**Pattern:** `[City]'s [Niche] Since [Year] — [Specific local proof]`

**Examples:**
- "Sacramento's Trusted Plumber Since 2006 — Over 3,000 Local Jobs Completed"
- "Roofing the Bay Area Since 1998 — From Palo Alto to San Jose"
- "Fresno's Go-To Electrician for 22 Years — 300+ Five-Star Reviews"
- "San Diego's Family-Owned HVAC Company — 15 Years, Same Neighborhood"

**Best for:** Homepage H1 alternative, About page headline, local trust-building sections

**Avoid:** Only use "since [year]" if the year is accurate. Don't round up years in business.

---

## Formula 11 — Benefit-First Feature

**Pattern:** `[The benefit you get] — Powered by [The feature that delivers it]`

**Examples:**
- "No Surprise Bills — Because We Quote Every Job in Writing Before We Start"
- "Faster Repairs — Because We Stock 500+ Parts on Every Truck"
- "Better Air Quality — Because We Clean the Coils, Not Just the Filter"
- "Longer-Lasting Roof — Because We Use Manufacturer-Certified Installation Methods"

**Best for:** Feature callout sections, "Why us" differentiator bullets elevated to headlines

**Avoid:** Don't lead with the feature — homeowners buy outcomes, not methods.

---

## Formula 12 — Risk Reversal

**Pattern:** `Try [Service] Risk-Free — [Specific guarantee]`
OR: `Zero Risk: [What we do if you're not happy]`

**Examples:**
- "Free Estimate — No Obligation, No Sales Pressure"
- "Zero Risk: If You're Not Happy With the Cleaning, We Come Back at No Charge"
- "First Service Call Free — No Trip Fee for New Customers"
- "Free Roof Inspection — We'll Tell You What We Find, Even If It's Nothing"

**Best for:** Homepage CTA, service pages CTA section, form page headline

**Avoid:** Don't promise "free" anything unless it's genuinely free with no upsell pressure. Visitors have seen "free estimate" used as a sales visit. Acknowledge this skepticism directly in supporting copy.

---

## Formula 13 — Social Proof Headline

**Pattern:** `"[Real customer quote]" — [Customer name, city]`
OR: `[Number] Sacramento Families Count on [Business Name] Every Year`

**Examples:**
- `"They showed up in 45 minutes and had my heat running before dinner." — Karen M., Elk Grove`
- `"First time I've ever had a plumber leave my bathroom cleaner than they found it." — David R., Folsom`
- "847 Sacramento Families Can't Be Wrong — 4.9 Stars on Google"
- "Trusted by [City] Homeowners for [X] Years Running"

**Best for:** Testimonials section headline, social proof callout boxes, trust bar text

**Avoid:** Never fabricate testimonials or quotes. Never use generic-sounding fake names. If real testimonials aren't available yet, use the number/rating formula instead.

---

## Formula 14 — Simple + Direct (High Urgency)

**Pattern:** `[Service]. [City]. [Phone]. [Available when].`

**Examples:**
- "Emergency Plumber. Sacramento. (916) 555-0100. Available Now."
- "AC Repair. Same Day. Fresno. Call (559) 555-0100."
- "Roof Leak? San Jose. (408) 555-0100. 24/7."

**Best for:** 404 pages, above-the-fold mobile strip, emergency landing pages, paid search landing pages

**Avoid:** This formula is too stripped-down for a main homepage H1 — use it for urgency contexts where the visitor just needs to know: can you help me, are you there, how do I call you.

---

## Formula 15 — Outcome Stacking

**Pattern:** `[Outcome 1]. [Outcome 2]. [Outcome 3]. That's [Service] from [Company].`

**Examples:**
- "On Time. Honest Pricing. Zero Mess Left Behind. That's How [Business Name] Works."
- "Licensed. Insured. Guaranteed. That's the [Business Name] Standard."
- "Repaired Right. Permitted Properly. Cleaned Up Completely. That's [Business Name]."
- "Same Crew. Same Time. Same High Standard. Every Week."

**Best for:** About page tagline, homepage "Why us" section, footer tagline

**Avoid:** Keep to 3 items maximum. Four or more stacked outcomes starts to sound like a compliance checklist, not a promise.

---

## Formula 16 — Before/After Contrast

**Pattern:** `Before: [The problem]. After: [The resolution + feeling].`

**Examples:**
- "Before: No hot water, a cold shower, and a full morning disrupted. After: Fixed same day, for $340, before you got home from work."
- "Before: A roof leak you've been ignoring. After: 10-year warranty, documented, photographed, and guaranteed."
- "Before: An overgrown yard you're embarrassed by. After: A lawn that makes the neighbors look."

**Best for:** Service page hero subheadline, testimonials context, case study or portfolio section headlines

**Avoid:** Don't overuse — one before/after contrast per page is usually sufficient.

---

## Formula 17 — Specificity Headline

**Pattern:** Replace vague claim with a specific number or detail.

| Vague (Don't use) | Specific (Use) |
|---|---|
| "Fast response" | "At your door in 45 minutes or less" |
| "Affordable pricing" | "Most repairs under $350" |
| "Many years of experience" | "18 years in Sacramento" |
| "Lots of satisfied customers" | "847 five-star reviews" |
| "Quality work" | "3-year labor warranty on every job" |
| "We'll take care of it" | "Fixed same day, 98% of the time" |

**Application:** This isn't a formula — it's an editing pass. Run every headline through the specificity filter before publishing. Every vague claim is a lost conversion opportunity.

---

## Formula 18 — CTA Headline (Action-Led)

**Pattern:** `[Action verb] Your [Specific outcome] Today — [Zero-friction next step]`

**Examples:**
- "Schedule Your Free Water Heater Estimate Today — Takes 60 Seconds"
- "Get Your Roof Inspection Booked — We Have Openings This Week"
- "Call for Same-Day Plumbing Help — Live Answer, No Voicemail"
- "Send Your Service Request — We Respond Within 2 Hours"

**Best for:** Above any contact form, CTA banner sections, bottom-of-page CTA blocks

**Avoid:** Never use "Submit" as a CTA verb. The CTA headline should describe the value the visitor receives, not the action they perform.

---

## Formula 19 — Pain Amplification (Use Sparingly)

**Pattern:** `[Problem] + [Consequence of inaction] → [Relief via this contractor]`

**Examples:**
- "A small roof leak gets expensive fast — stop it before it reaches the drywall."
- "Running an old furnace all winter costs more than fixing it in fall."
- "Ignored electrical issues don't fix themselves. They become house fires."
- "Deferred plumbing problems become emergency plumbing bills."

**Best for:** Service page supporting copy, blog post intros, seasonal maintenance reminder sections

**Avoid:** Use this formula once per page maximum. Repeating pain language without relief feels manipulative and damages trust. Always follow with the relief/solution.

---

## Formula 20 — Community Authority

**Pattern:** `Proud to Serve [Specific Area/Community Reference]`
OR: `[Local landmark / area name] Residents Know [Business Name]`

**Examples:**
- "Proudly Serving East Sacramento, Midtown, and the River Park Neighborhood Since 2006"
- "Land Park Families Have Trusted [Business Name] Since [Year]"
- "From Elk Grove to Roseville — Sacramento's Plumber"
- "Part of the [City] Community Since [Year]"

**Best for:** Footer tagline, About page intro, service area pages

**Avoid:** Generic "serving the greater [metro] area" language. Name specific neighborhoods when possible — it signals local knowledge and builds community trust.

---

## Quick Reference: Headline Test

Before publishing any headline, run this 10-second test:

```
1. Is the service or outcome clear? Yes/No
2. Is there a location signal? Yes/No
3. Is there a trust or proof element? Yes/No
4. Does it start with an action or outcome (not "We" or "Welcome")? Yes/No
5. Could this exact headline appear on a competitor's site unchanged? Yes = rewrite
```

Pass = 4 out of 5. Fail = rewrite.
