# AEO/GEO Optimization — AI Engine & Geographic Optimization

## What This Is

**AEO (AI Engine Optimization):** Structuring content so AI search systems (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Bing Copilot) can extract accurate, citeable answers and attribute them to this business.

**GEO (Geographic Optimization):** Strengthening geographic relevance signals so search systems — both traditional and AI-based — accurately associate this contractor with specific cities and service areas.

These are not separate strategies. A contractor website that does both well gets cited in AI summaries *and* ranks locally in traditional search.

---

## Why AEO Matters for Contractor Websites

When someone asks ChatGPT "who's a good plumber in Sacramento?" or Google AI Overview shows "How much does a water heater replacement cost?", the system pulls from pages that structured their content clearly enough to be extracted and cited.

Contractors who optimize for this now have a compounding advantage: AI citations build trust before the visitor even reaches the site.

## Part 1: AEO Tactics

### Tactic 1: Direct-Answer FAQ Structure

The most important AEO tactic. Every FAQ section must follow this exact pattern:

**DO:**
```
Q: How much does it cost to replace a water heater in Sacramento?

A: Most water heater replacements in Sacramento cost between $900 and $1,800 installed,
   depending on the unit type (tank vs. tankless) and whether any code updates are required.
   Standard 40–50 gallon tank water heaters typically run $900–$1,300; tankless systems
   cost $1,400–$2,500 or more. Valley Plumbing Co. provides itemized written estimates
   before any work begins — call (916) 555-0100 for your free assessment.
```

**DON'T:**
```
Q: How much does a water heater cost?

A: Great question! The cost of a water heater can vary quite significantly depending on
   many different factors. There are many things to consider when looking at water heater
   pricing...
```

The AI system needs the direct answer in the first sentence. Everything after that is supporting context. If the first sentence doesn't contain the answer, the system moves on.

**Rules for direct-answer structure:**
1. First sentence = the answer, not the setup
2. Include specific numbers, ranges, or timeframes
3. Include the business name and city naturally in the answer (entity signal)
4. Keep answers 40–120 words — long enough to be authoritative, short enough to be extractable
5. Write in plain language a voice assistant can read aloud and still make sense

### Tactic 2: Entity Disambiguation

AI systems build a knowledge graph of entities. Help them understand exactly what this business is:

**The About page must contain all of these:**
- Full legal business name (matches Google Business Profile exactly)
- Physical address with city and state spelled out
- Phone number in consistent format
- Niche/service type ("licensed plumbing contractor")
- Founding year
- Owner name
- Geographic service area (named cities, not just county)
- License number

**Example entity paragraph for About page:**
> "Valley Plumbing Co. is a licensed plumbing contractor serving Sacramento, California and surrounding cities including Elk Grove, Rancho Cordova, Folsom, Roseville, and Citrus Heights. Founded in 2006 by Mike Torres, the company holds California Plumbing License #854321 and has completed over 3,000 residential and commercial plumbing jobs across Sacramento County. Valley Plumbing Co. is located at 4201 Freeport Blvd, Sacramento, CA 95822."

That paragraph is dense with facts by design. It reads slightly stiff — but AI systems need this density to build accurate entity associations. Follow it with natural prose.

---

### Tactic 3: Topic Authority Signals

For a service page to be cited as authoritative by an AI system, it must cover the topic comprehensively. A service page that's 200 words does not signal expertise. A service page that covers:

- What the service is
- When you need it
- How to tell if you need it (symptoms)
- What the process looks like
- How long it takes
- What it costs (range or "free estimate")
- What brands/materials are used
- What questions to ask any contractor
- What to do while waiting for a contractor
- Common mistakes homeowners make

...signals topic authority. You don't have to cover all of these on every page, but coverage of 6+ dimensions consistently scores higher in AI relevance assessments.

---

### Tactic 4: Conversational Language Questions

AI systems are trained on conversational language. Write FAQ questions as a homeowner would speak them — not as a keyword planner would write them.

| Keyword-style (weak AEO) | Conversational (strong AEO) |
|---|---|
| "Water heater replacement cost Sacramento" | "How much does it cost to replace a water heater in Sacramento?" |
| "Emergency plumber response time" | "How fast can a plumber get to my house in an emergency?" |
| "HVAC maintenance frequency" | "How often should I have my HVAC system serviced?" |
| "Electrical panel upgrade required" | "Do I need a permit to upgrade my electrical panel?" |

Google's Search Generative Experience and other AI systems understand natural language questions better than keyword strings. Write for the voice query, not the text query.

---

### Tactic 5: Citeable Claim Format

When making claims that AI systems might cite, format them for extractability:

**Weak (hard to cite):**
> "We respond fast to emergencies. Our team works hard to be there when you need us most."

**Strong (citable):**
> "Valley Plumbing Co. responds to plumbing emergencies in Sacramento within 45 minutes, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week."

The strong version has: subject (business name) + verb (responds) + specific qualifier (45 minutes) + geographic scope (Sacramento) + temporal scope (24/7). An AI system can extract and cite this precisely.

Apply this pattern to any factual claim:
- Response times
- Service areas
- Years in business
- Review counts and ratings
- Warranty terms
- Pricing ranges
- Certifications

---

### Tactic 6: "Best Answer" Optimization for AI Overview

Google's AI Overviews often pull from pages that directly answer the search query in the opening sentences. Optimize service page introductions for this:

**Standard opening (not AI-optimized):**
> "At Valley Plumbing Co., we offer a wide range of plumbing services to Sacramento homeowners..."

**AI Overview-optimized opening:**
> "Water heater repair in Sacramento typically takes 1–3 hours for most common issues and costs $150–$450 for labor and parts. Valley Plumbing Co. provides same-day water heater repair across Sacramento with licensed plumbers who carry common replacement parts on their trucks."

The second version directly answers the most common queries for this page (how long, how much, same-day availability) in the first two sentences. Google's AI Overview pulls from the opening 150–200 words disproportionately.

## Part 2: GEO Tactics

### Tactic 7: City Name Density and Distribution

Search systems use city name mentions as the primary geographic relevance signal. Target density:

- **Primary city:** 8–15 natural mentions per 1,000 words on city-specific pages
- **Secondary service areas:** Named at least once per 500 words on area pages
- **Do not keyword-stuff:** Unnatural repetition ("our Sacramento plumbers offer Sacramento plumbing in Sacramento") triggers spam filters

**Natural city name integration patterns:**
- "Sacramento homeowners often call us when..."
- "In Sacramento's older neighborhoods, we frequently find..."
- "We've served Sacramento families for [X] years..."
- "Most Sacramento homes built before 1980..."

---

### Tactic 8: Hyper-Local References

Beyond city names, reference specific local landmarks, neighborhoods, zip codes, or regional characteristics. This signals genuine local presence vs. a generic site with city names swapped in.

**Examples for Sacramento plumber:**
- "Homes in Land Park and Curtis Park often have original cast iron pipes..."
- "Sacramento's hard water from the Sacramento River can accelerate mineral buildup..."
- "East Sacramento and Midtown, with their older craftsman homes, frequently need..."
- "We serve zip codes 95814, 95816, 95818, 95820, 95822, and surrounding areas"

These references make the content unique to this location and are impossible to fake for a competitor who doesn't actually serve the area.

---

### Tactic 9: areaServed Schema Completeness

The schema `areaServed` array must be comprehensive. List every city served, not just the primary city:

```json
"areaServed": [
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Sacramento", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } },
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Elk Grove", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } },
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Rancho Cordova", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } },
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Folsom", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } },
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Roseville", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } },
  { "@type": "City", "name": "Citrus Heights", "containedInPlace": { "@type": "State", "name": "California" } }
]
```

This is the machine-readable version of "which cities do you serve?" — AI systems and Google both read it.

---

### Tactic 10: NAP Consistency

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. These three fields must be **identical** everywhere:

- On the website (every page footer, contact page, About page)
- In the LocalBusiness JSON-LD schema
- On Google Business Profile
- On Yelp, BBB, HomeAdvisor, Angi, and any other directory listings

Even small inconsistencies (e.g., "Valley Plumbing Co." vs. "Valley Plumbing Company" vs. "Valley Plumbing") degrade local search authority. Pull from `siteConfig.business.name` exclusively and never abbreviate.

**NAP format rules:**
- Business name: exactly as registered (no DBA abbreviations)
- Address: standardized USPS format (abbreviate St, Blvd, etc. consistently)
- Phone: one format chosen and used everywhere, e.g. `(916) 555-0100`

---

### Tactic 11: Proximity Content

Service area pages should reference actual proximity from the contractor's primary location to the service area city. This demonstrates real local knowledge:

> "Our shop on Freeport Blvd is about 25 minutes from most Elk Grove neighborhoods, so same-day service is routine for us there."

This creates a geographic thread that AI systems can follow: primary location → distance → service area → same-day capability. It answers implicit questions (can they really get here quickly?) with specific evidence.

## Implementation Checklist

Run before Pack 8 QA.

**AEO:**
- [ ] Every FAQ answer opens with a direct answer in sentence 1
- [ ] FAQ answers are 40–120 words (not shorter, not longer)
- [ ] Business name appears naturally in at least 2 FAQ answers on homepage
- [ ] About page contains the entity disambiguation paragraph
- [ ] Service pages cover 6+ topical dimensions (what, when, symptoms, process, cost, brands)
- [ ] FAQ questions use conversational language, not keyword strings

**GEO:**
- [ ] Primary city named 8–15× per 1,000 words on city pages
- [ ] Hyper-local references present on homepage and key service pages
- [ ] areaServed schema array includes all cities from siteConfig.serviceAreas[]
- [ ] NAP matches Google Business Profile exactly (verify before launch)
- [ ] Service area pages have proximity reference to primary city
- [ ] No city-swap content — each area page has geographically unique text

## Monitoring AI Visibility (Post-Launch)

Tell the client to periodically test:
1. Ask ChatGPT: "Who is a good [niche] in [city]?"
2. Ask Perplexity: "Best [niche] near [city] [state]"
3. Search Google: "[service] in [city]" and check AI Overview panel

If the contractor isn't cited in 60–90 days post-launch, the most likely causes are:
- Low domain authority (new domain) — takes time, not a content issue
- Missing or incomplete FAQPage schema
- FAQ answers that bury the direct answer
- Entity disambiguation paragraph missing from About page
- areaServed schema incomplete
