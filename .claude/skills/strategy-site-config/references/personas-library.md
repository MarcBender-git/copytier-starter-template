# Personas Library — Contractor Website Visitors

Pre-built persona templates for contractor websites. Use these to prioritize which sections
appear above the fold, which CTAs to emphasize, and how to frame trust signals.

---

## The 4 Universal Personas

Every contractor website serves these four visitor types. Their needs differ significantly.

---

### Persona 1: The Emergency Caller
**"I have a crisis. I need someone NOW."**

**Demographics:** Any age. Homeowner or renter. Discovered the problem in the last hour.

**Behavior:**
- Found you via "emergency plumber near me" or similar urgent search
- On mobile — probably crouched in front of the problem
- Will not scroll past the fold. Will not read paragraphs.
- Calls immediately if the number is visible. Leaves if it isn't.
- Ignores pricing, reviews, about pages — they're past that

**What they look for:**
- Phone number visible without scrolling
- "Available now" or "24/7" language
- Human answers (not voicemail)
- Approximate arrival time

**What converts them:**
- Sticky mobile call bar
- Phone number in hero, 40px+
- "Emergency Service Available" badge
- Zero-friction call experience

**CTA preference:** Phone call only. Forms are too slow.

---

### Persona 2: The Research Comparer
**"I'm getting 3 quotes. Your site is one of them."**

**Demographics:** 35-60, homeowner, project-minded, has time to compare.

**Behavior:**
- Has 2-4 contractor tabs open simultaneously
- Spending 2-4 minutes per site
- Reading reviews, scanning credentials, comparing prices/process
- Will call the one that feels most professional and trustworthy

**What they look for:**
- Specific service descriptions (not vague "quality service" language)
- Real named reviews with dates
- License numbers, years in business, team photos
- Clear process explanation (what happens when I call?)
- Pricing transparency (ranges are fine — silence is suspicious)

**What converts them:**
- Trust bar section (rating + reviews + license + years)
- Named testimonials with city and service
- Process section (3-5 step visual flow)
- Competitive differentiator in hero subtext

**CTA preference:** Contact form or call — they're comfortable with both.

---

### Persona 3: The Price Shopper
**"What's the cheapest option that won't screw me?"**

**Demographics:** 25-45, budget-conscious, may be renters or recent homeowners.

**Behavior:**
- Searching for prices first, provider second
- Will bounce if no pricing signal at all
- Responds to "free estimate" language
- Susceptible to limited-time offers and guarantees

**What they look for:**
- "Free estimate" or "free quote" language
- Financing options (especially roofing, HVAC, GC)
- Satisfaction guarantees
- "No surprise fees" or "upfront pricing" messaging

**What converts them:**
- "Get a Free Estimate" as the primary CTA (not "Call Us")
- Guarantee badge
- Financing mention if applicable
- Social proof that justifies the price (reviews)

**CTA preference:** Form (low commitment) or call if there's a clear free-estimate hook.

---

### Persona 4: The Returning Customer
**"I used you before. I need you again."**

**Demographics:** Any. Already trusts the business. Quick decision.

**Behavior:**
- Navigates directly to contact page or calls
- May search "[Company Name] phone number" to find the site
- Skips most content — straight to contact
- Will refer others if reminded with a "tell a friend" prompt

**What they look for:**
- Easy way to reach the same person or company
- Confirmation that business is still operating
- Hours, updated contact info

**What converts them:**
- Contact page loads fast and has all options (phone, form, address)
- Nothing has changed that would create friction

**CTA preference:** Phone call.

## Niche-Specific Persona Extensions

Certain niches have an additional dominant persona type.

### Plumbing: "The DIY Failure"
Started a repair project, made it worse. Embarrassed. Needs help today.
- Lead with "No judgment" framing in hero
- Acknowledge the specific failed DIY scenario in FAQ
- Emphasize "We've seen it all — just call"

### HVAC & Cleaning: "The Property Manager"
Manages 5-50 rental units. Wants a reliable vendor for recurring work.
- Mention commercial/multi-unit in services
- Emphasize reliability and consistency
- Offer to set up recurring service accounts
- Biggest long-term revenue opportunity per client

### Roofing: "The Insurance Claimant"
Recent storm damage. Has an insurance claim in progress. Confused.
- Lead with "We work with your insurance company"
- Use "storm damage" language prominently in hero
- Explain the claims process in FAQ
- Emphasize documentation and photos

### Landscaping: "The New Homeowner"
Just bought a house. Yard is neglected or doesn't match their vision.
- Lead with transformation framing ("Before / After")
- Offer initial consultation as low-commitment entry point
- Show portfolio photos prominently
- Emphasize weekly maintenance as a recurring relationship

## Persona → Homepage Section Priority

Which persona's primary job is addressed by which section, in order of page scroll:

| Section | Primary Persona | What it resolves |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| Hero | Emergency Caller | Job 4 — contact right now |
| Trust Bar | Research Comparer | Job 3 — credentials fast |
| Services Grid | Research Comparer + Price Shopper | Job 1 — confirm coverage |
| Why Choose Us | Research Comparer | Job 3 — differentiation |
| Testimonials | Research Comparer + Price Shopper | Job 3 — social proof |
| Service Area Map/List | All | Job 2 — geographic confirmation |
| Process Section | Research Comparer | Job 3 — reduces uncertainty |
| FAQ | Research Comparer + Price Shopper | Jobs 1+3 — objection handling |
| CTA Section | All | Job 4 — final conversion push |
| Footer NAP | Returning Customer | Job 4 — quick re-contact |

## Persona → CTA Preference Summary

| Persona | Primary CTA | Secondary CTA | Never |
|---------|------------|---------------|-------|
| Emergency Caller | Phone (click-to-call) | — | Form |
| Research Comparer | Phone or Form | "Get a Free Estimate" | Chatbot |
| Price Shopper | "Free Estimate" Form | Phone | Pricing page (doesn't exist) |
| Returning Customer | Phone | Contact page | Anywhere else |
| Property Manager | Form (needs documentation) | Phone | — |
| Insurance Claimant | Phone | Form with "storm damage" label | — |
