## How Leads Come In

There are two ways a customer contacts you through the website:

### Way 1: Contact Form (Most Common)

When someone fills out the "Request a Quote" form on your website:

1. They submit the form with their name, phone number, and what they need
2. **Within 60 seconds**, you receive an email at **[CONTRACTOR_EMAIL]** with all their details
3. The lead is also saved in a backup database (explained below)
4. **Call them back within 30 minutes** — research shows this increases your chance of booking the job by 5 times. After 2 hours, most people have already called someone else.

The email you receive looks like this:

> **New Lead from [COMPANY_NAME] Website**
>
> Name: Alex Johnson
> Phone: (555) 234-5678
> Email: alex@email.com
> Service: Water Heater Repair
> Message: My water heater stopped working. Need someone today if possible.
> Submitted: March 18, 2026 at 2:34 PM

**Suggested response script:**
> "Hi, this is [your name] from [company name]. I'm calling back about your
> inquiry from our website. How can I help you today?"

### Way 2: Direct Phone Call

Some people skip the form and call directly. Your phone number is displayed
prominently at the top of every page, and there's a "Call Now" button at the
bottom of the screen on mobile.

When someone calls directly, they don't go through a form — your phone just rings.
These are often your hottest leads because they want to talk to someone right now.

## A Note About Google

Your new website is optimized to rank on Google, but rankings take time.
Here's what to expect:

**Week 1–2:** Google discovers your site and starts indexing it
**Month 1–3:** You may start appearing for searches, especially local searches
**Month 3–6:** Rankings stabilize; you should see consistent traffic
**Month 6+:** Continued growth as the site builds authority

Things that help Google rank you faster:
1. **Get reviews on Google** — ask every happy customer to leave a review
2. **Keep your Google Business Profile updated** — photos, hours, and posts help
3. **Tell people about your website** — the more people link to it, the better

Things that hurt rankings (avoid these):
- Inconsistent NAP (your business name, address, phone should be identical everywhere)
- Buying fake reviews
- Keyword stuffing in your Google Business Profile description

## Glossary — Plain English Definitions

**Domain name:** Your website address (e.g. joeplumbing.com). You own this and
pay to renew it annually.

**Hosting:** The service that keeps your website running online 24 hours a day.
Copytier manages this for you.

**Contact form:** The form on your website where customers fill in their info
to request a quote. Submissions come to your email.

**Lead:** A potential customer who has shown interest in your services.

**Analytics:** Data about how many people visit your site, where they came from,
and what they do when they're there.

**SEO:** Search Engine Optimization — the work that helps your site appear higher
in Google search results.

**SSL:** The padlock icon in the browser address bar. Shows that your site is
secure and customer data is encrypted. Your site has this.
